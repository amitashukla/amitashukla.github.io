Product Design

wAIR

Mar 2017

The opening of a bus depot in Upper Harlem caused many children of the community to suddenly contract asthma due to the severe air contamination it caused. My Design for America project team and I created a wearable device that tracks the ambient air pollution its user is exposed to over the course of the day. We intended it as an aid in lobbying for the community residents affected by the pollution.

Equal Solar

Feb 2017

In partnership with the Harlem community organization WeACT for Environmental Justice, and with Design for America, my team and I created a prototype for a portable solar charger to raise awareness about solar power and to convince community residents to adopt solar power. Team members for this project include Columbia students Sophie Stadler, Noah Gallant, Kevin Le, Nikhil Jain, and myself Amita Shukla. My role in particular was to create mockups of our project in SketchUp and to create flyers for the local community center to distribute

U.N. Initiative Web Design

Aug 2016-Feb 2017

This work was part of the United Nations Harmony with Nature Initiative, under the Department of Economic and Social Affairs. I worked under U.N. Sustainable Development Officer Maria Mercedes Sanchez. This was part of U.N. Resolution A/RES/65/164. The initiative tries to create international law about 'rights of the earth', or, earth jurisprudence. I worked on a forum for scientists and policymakers to interact and have a virtual dialogue with each other. During the time I was with the initiative, I saw half-a-dozen laws passed, including one in the Brazilian constitution.
